%%1%%Skill Of Focus: Squat-on Vault!  Pre-Flight Extension

1.  Squat-on vault on 3-trap stack.  (You may begin with a 2-trap stack if necessary, but move to a 3-trap stack as soon as their jump gets strong!  You can place karate blocker in front of the SB to hurdle over!)  Run from a short distance from a spot marker back from the SB.  Squat on vault, land on feet on trap.  Jump off trap, land in tight SLP, “Finish”.
POD: Two foot take off from SB, hands on X’s, jump feet to lines.  Can they balance in a tight finish until you clap your hands?!  This is great for body control and actually finishing the movement!

2.  Same with a longer, faster run
POD: Child should still be in control of their body and the move!  Make sure they continue to jump off the SB with two feet!

3.  Same with pre-flight.  Move the SB back so the child can get their bodies into an extended body position (Pre-flight) before they make contact with the traps.  Add the karate blockers between the SB and the traps.  How many can they extend over?  “Can you turn the blockers parallel end-to-end in line with the run?  Now how many can you vault over?”
POD: Tight body is a must during the pre-flight!  If they get loosey-goosy, take a Karate blocker out and start building the pre-flight again.  Child should be looking at the X’s when leaving the SB.  This can be scary!  You need to spot between the SB and the traps to make sure the child is safe!

4.  Squat-through vault

%%2%%Pullover with Walk Up Wall (Review)

1.  Kids can practice their pullover with wall to walk up
POD: strong kick initiating the pullover, switch of the wrists to get chest up

2.  Same, with only one walk on wall before the kick over.  You can tape a line on the CH for their one step.  Slightly move bar away from wall to get more extension.

%%3%%Conditioning on High Beam
Shout out a different conditioning activity for the kids to do every few minutes!  You might want to briefly stop the group and explain and demonstrate the Pommel Horse Drill when that time comes!

-  Handstand Travels down beam: feet on beam, hands on mat

-  Support Travels: straight arm support position on beam.  Try to take 5-10 sideways steps with hands!

-  “Sloth-Ups”: do a sloth hang under the beam (grab the beam with hands, wrap legs around beam); pull up with your arms and touch a shoulder to the beam – down; now the other shoulder to the beam – down.  Do 10 of them!

-  Pommel Horse Drill: Leg Cuts from a support!  From a front support on the side of the beam (legs and arms straight), try to lift one hand as you swing leg to the side and to the front in one fast motion!  Now cut it back!  Try the other leg!

3.  If beam is near the group mat, create a down and back tumbling course before returning to the vaulting station.  On the way down, have the children practice HST, CW’s and F. Rolls (ex: F. Roll: straight body, reach over beanbag, F. Roll).  Instructors should keep a watchful eye and provide SPF to keep the children engaged and on task!
